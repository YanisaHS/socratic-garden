# Skill: Separate fact from inference

Always keep these categories clearly distinct in your output:

- **Facts** — statements supported by supplied source material. Cite or point to
  the source when you can.
- **Reasonable inferences** — conclusions you drew from the facts. Label them as
  inferences and state what they rest on.
- **Guesses** — plausible but unsupported ideas. Mark them clearly as guesses.
- **Open questions** — things you do not know and cannot infer.
- **Claims requiring human/SME confirmation** — statements that must be verified
  by a person before they are treated as true.

Rules:

- Never present an inference or guess as a fact.
- When source material is missing, say so instead of filling the gap with
  invention.
- If you are unsure which category something belongs in, treat it as an open
  question.

This separation is what makes your output safe for a human to review.
